Tour Details

Itinerary
Morning: Jungle Boss headquarters - Elephant cave

Around 8:00 AM, your guide will depart to pick you up from your accommodation and bring you to the Jungle Boss office. Here, you will receive necessary items such as water, towels, etc., listen to information about the tour itinerary, safety precautions, and sign the Release form. Afterward, the car will transport you deep into the core zone of Phong Nha - Ke Bang National Park to the starting point.

 

Here, the group will follow the trail leading to the entrance of Elephant Cave. Hidden behind the lush foliage of the Ke Bang forest, a massive cave entrance gradually reveals itself before your eyes, accompanied by a refreshing cool breeze. You will take a short break to recharge with snacks and pastries. Then, Jungle Boss's safety assistants will help you equip helmets with headlamps and gloves to begin exploring this cave.

 

You will navigate terrain covered in green moss, weave through sparkling stalactites and stalagmites in the darkness, and listen to the history of the cave's formation and historical stories about a glorious wartime period, when Elephant Cave served as a stronghold for Vietnamese soldiers during the resistance war. This fascinating journey is sure to take you from one impressive surprise to another.

Lunchtime: Elephant cave - Ma Da valley

After exploring Elephant Cave and taking photos at the back entrance, you will return and continue to the campsite in Ma Da Valley. When you are feeling tired, you will be instantly "refreshed" by swimming in the crystal-clear, cool Ma Da Lake.

 

Lunch will be prepared by the lakeside, featuring delicious grilled dishes prepared by Jungle Boss's chefs. Accompanied by the specialty "Moi Cheo" (a special salt mixture) of the Phong Nha region, the meal will undoubtedly leave a lasting impression on you.

Afternoon: Ma Da valley - Tra Ang valley - Jungle Boss headquaters

After lunch, you will continue trekking through the jungle to Tra Ang Valley - home to the Tra Ang water cave, which is over 600 meters long. After arriving at the campsite, safety assistants will help you wear life jackets and headlamps to explore this unique water cave.

 

Upon completion, you will return to the campsite to enjoy hot tea or coffee and continue trekking for approximately 15 minutes back to Road 20 Quyet Thang. Here, the car will be waiting to transport you back to the Jungle Boss office, concluding the journey.

Campsites

Bathing

The campsite on Jungle Boss tours is set up near water sources such as lakes, rivers, and streams. The water in these areas is mostly clean and clear year-round. You are free to swim and bathe comfortably in these areas. Our safety assistants will always be on hand to assist and support you in any situation.

To protect the natural environment, the use of chemical shampoos or shower gels is not permitted on Jungle Boss adventure tours. We provide biodegradable and environmentally friendly shampoo throughout the tour.

Toilet

Jungle Boss uses composting toilets for all adventure tours. The toilet is set up in a toilet tent, inside there will be a toilet with a standard seat and a bucket for waste. After using the toilet, please use the provided rice husk to cover the waste in the bucket to prevent bacteria and odor. Toilet paper and hand sanitizer are placed right next to it. Each Jungle Boss toilet tent has an air freshener to eliminate odors and create a pleasant scent.

Please always use this toilet and do not defecate in nature to avoid affecting the ecosystem.

Weather Condition

Phong Nha Ke Bang has 4 seasons with Spring, Summer, Autumn and Winter, it also experiences a monsoon tropical climate, with two distinct seasons: the rainy season and the dry season. The rainy season spans from September to March, while the dry season stretches from April to August. During the rainy season, temperatures can drop as low as 17C, with January being the coldest month. In contrast, the dry season can bring higher temperatures, with May usually recording the highest temperatures, but most of it is reduced when visitors are in the jungle and the caves exploring, as it might drop to the temperature around 23C.

When you visit Phong Nha in each season, you'll always witness a unique beauty. If you choose to visit during the rainy season, the waterfalls in the jungle and the calcite lakes inside the caves will be exceptionally beautiful and full, allowing you to capture memorable photos. On the other hand, if you opt for the dry season, your trekking routes in the jungle will be drier and more manageable. Throughout the year, we are at our most beautiful status from April to May when the weather is dry, cool, and the scenery is at its finest.

INFORMATION ABOUT Elephant Cave & Ma Da Valley Jungle Trek 1D

After exploring Elephant Cave, the team will hike the jungle trail, cross rivers, and balance across fallen trees. Deep into Ma Da Valley you and the team will go until you reach the heavenly blue waters of Ma Da Crystal Lake.

Here you and the team will have the opportunity to take a swim, do some cliff jumping, and enjoy a delicious Jungle BBQ prepared by your porter (vegan options also available). With full bellies and feeling refreshed you and the team will gear up and continue on the jungle hike.

A few kilometers down the trail and a couple of river crossings later the team will come upon a large bend in the river and a staging area for the swim into Tra Ang Cave. After a short scramble up some limestone rocks, you will reach the cool waters of Tra Ang Cave and begin the 600m swim into the furthest depths of the cave.
